Sushruta

An ancient Indian physician

First Cosmetic Surgeon

It is believed that Sushruta was born in Varanasi,India in around 800 BCE.The literal meaning of his name is 'well heard',and undoubtedly he was well-learnt and a great physician.

It is expounded by Kashiraj Divodas Dhanvantari and compiled by Sushruta, who was the son of Sage Vishvamitra, and disciple of Divodas Dhanvantari, the King of Kashi. As per the Dalhaṇa Acharya, commentator of Sushrutasaṁhita, Sage Nagarjuna was the 'Pratisaṁskarta' or redactor of this Sushrutasaṁhita. Emendation or Paṭhashuddhi, of Sushrutasaṁhita was done by Candraṭa, the son of Tīsaṭa. Divōdāsa and his disciple Suśruta are historical sage physicians probably belong to 1500 B.C and 1000 B.C., Sushruta renowned as the "Father of Surgery".

The Sushruta Samhita, a treatise ascribed to him, is one of the most important surviving ancient treatises on medicine and is considered a foundational text of Ayurveda.The treatise addresses all aspects of general medicine, but the impressive chapters on surgery have led to the false impression that this is its main topic. The translator G. D. Singhal dubbed Sushruta "the father of surgery" on account of these detailed accounts of surgery.

The treatise mainly focuses on various surgeries and their methods.It also discuses Vayu,Pitta and Kapha,so-called humours of the body
